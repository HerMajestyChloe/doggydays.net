How to teach your dog to jump through a hoop!

Are you ready for another wonderful lesson in the art of dog training? I sure am! Today we will be taking the plunge into our first trick! How exciting! Today we will be teaching your pup to jump through a hoop. Sounds intimidating, right? It’s actually MUCH easier than it sounds, dogs can pick up on this rather easily. Imagine the looks on your friends and family when your pooch starts jumping through a hoop you are holding your friends and family will all be in awe. Most people believe this to be an advanced trick, so don’t be surprised if they ask YOU to teach THEIR dog!

This exercise is great for bonding with your pup as well as a great exercise for them as well. If you have a breed that is highly energetic, adding this into your daily routine might help them tucker out a little easier. Some dog breeds require hours of rigorous exercise a day, making a little course in the backyard could be a great way to help them burn off some energy as well as give them a mental challenge. This is a great first trick to learn because it encourages teamwork, exercise, and play! You can also easily add this to a full-blown agility course if you are handy enough. You’d be surprised how easy and cheap it can be to make a course for your pet.

Depending on the age of your puppy you may want to put this exercise on the back burner. For instance, an 8-week-old puppy may do lots of jumping, but that doesn’t mean they are ready for this. I would recommend waiting until the dog is at least 5 to 6 months old at a minimum. If they are an older dog, make sure they are in good health. You should be having a vet take a close look at your doggos health every 6 months to a year regardless of if they are sick or not. For instance, if your dog’s hip is out of place, or if they have arthritis, they will find it very hard and PAINFUL to perform this activity.

Remember, and I can’t stress this part to you enough. Dogs are family first, performers second. If your dog is uncomfortable with doing this exercise do not force It onto them. They need to know you are there for them. If they aren’t grasping it on the first try, that’s okay! It may take a few days or weeks of working with your pup to get them to the level that you want them to be at. Don’t raise your voice or yell if they are not doing what you want them to do as this will lead to an even harder time down the road. Remember to take your time, remain calm, and be firm. Have fun with it, it’s just as much a learning experience for you as it is for them, YOU GOT THIS!

To start this exercise, you are going to need to make a run to the dollar store and purchase a Hula Hoop. Don’t bother buying an expensive $60 professional hoop for your dog to jump through as there is no point in spending that kind of money on something you can get for a dollar. You’ll find that there is no difference between the two other than color. If you have kids, you may be able to skip this step and simply borrow their hula hoop if they have one. Make sure to ask them if it’s okay to borrow it first! Who knows, they may even want to help you train the dog. It may be really fun for you and your children if you include them in the training as well! My kids love to hold the hoop for our dog as he prances his way through them with grace.

Now that you have your hoop, and your dog is in good health we are ready to begin! Make sure to have your spirits high, your clicker ready, and your treats in hand. For this particular exercise, it’s best to be outside or in a room with lots of open space. If you have wooden floors your dog may have a hard time keeping its traction. Imagine your dog trying to get a running start and sliding straight into a wall. Outside will typically be better for exercises like this one to ensure nothing gets broken! If you ignore this and do it inside anyway, DO NOT yell at your dog if something ends up broken as it’s your own fault, you’ve been warned.

Let’s get started!

Step 1: Position the hoop on the floor in front of your dog. With a yummy treat in your main hand, lure your dog into the hoop. When the dog is standing in the center, immediately click your clicker, praise them, and give them a treat! Do this a few times until they start doing it without hesitating.

Step 2: Using your other hand, raise the hoop to where the bottom of the circle is touching the ground. With your main hand use the treat to guide them through the hoop! When they walk through, slick your clicker, praise them, and give them a treat. Do this until your dog is comfortable walking through the hoop.

Step 3: Now it’s time to spice things up! Lift your hoop a few inches from the ground. When they go through the hoop click your clicker and praise them. Every other time they go through rewarding them with a treat.

At this point, when your dog is jumping through the hoop consistently it’s time to make it a little harder!

Step 4: While taking your dog’s size into consideration, slowly raise the hoop higher, and higher with each pass-through. Be careful not to go too high. You can’t expect a Yorkie to jump 6 feet in the air.

The more your pet does this the better they will get. Eventually, if you want to increase the difficulty, ad a second or third hoop get the whole family involved and watch as your fur baby puts on an act! This should go without saying but leave the flaming hoops to the professionals! If you are lacking a hoop but your pet has learned this trick you can try forming a hop with your arms! This is always a fun way to get more involved with your pet as well as get some additional bonding time in.
